Frontier Firm Framework

Three phases of AI transformation

A Frontier Firm is where humans and AI agents work together as teams. Microsoft defines three phases of this journey.

Click each phase card to explore what it looks like in practice.

1
Phase 1 · Foundation
Human with Assistant

Copilot augments individual work. Employees use AI to draft, summarise and research — but it's a personal tool, not yet part of shared team workflows.

Explore Phase 1 →
Phase 1 · Foundation
What this looks like in practice
Copilot is deployed but usage varies widely — heavy users sit alongside those who rarely open it
AI helps with individual tasks — drafting emails, summarising meetings, one-off searches
No consistent team-level routines or delegated workflows yet
Agents exist but are mostly personal or unused beyond their creator
← Flip back
2
Phase 2 · Expansion
Human-Agent Teams

Teams delegate tasks to AI agents embedded in real workflows. Human-agent collaboration becomes a repeating pattern — not a one-off experiment.

Explore Phase 2 →
Phase 2 · Expansion
What this looks like in practice
Specific teams have agents embedded in daily workflows — not just experimenting
Humans delegate defined tasks — research, triage, note-taking — to agents on a routine basis
Some agents are shared across teams with high multi-session, high-return usage
Habits are forming but not yet consistent across the full workforce
← Flip back
3
Phase 3 · Frontier
Human-Led, Agent-Operated

AI agents run enterprise workflows end-to-end. Humans set direction and govern outcomes — agents execute. AI is embedded in how the organisation operates.

Explore Phase 3 →
Phase 3 · Frontier
What this looks like in practice
Agents execute complete workflows — onboarding, incident response, reporting — with minimal human intervention
A governed agent catalogue spans all major business functions with active Keep/Review/Retire cycles
70%+ of licensed users are active; most interact with 3+ agents across multiple workflows
Humans focus on strategy, exception-handling, and governance — not execution
← Flip back

We measure progress across three observable signals:

Spread it across the org
Reach

AI becoming a capability everyone relies on — not just a few power users. Measures whether AI reaches across roles, teams, and functions.

Turn use into routine
Habit

Building AI into the daily rhythm of work. Measures whether people are converting from experimenters into embedded, habitual users.

Push past the basics
Skill

Moving beyond narrow, assistive use into sophisticated territory — directing agents, multi-step workflows, AI as a collaborator not just a shortcut.
